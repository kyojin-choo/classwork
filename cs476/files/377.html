1. Kulfold

[Jelcin]

Az oroszorszagi elnokvalasztasokat Borisz Jelcin nyerte, igy tovabbra
is o marad az allamfo. A szavazatok 99 szazalekanak osszesitese
alapjan tamogatottsaga megkozeliti az 54 szazalekot, mig Gennagyij
Zjuganov 40,5 szazalekot ert el. A reszvetel 67 szazalekos volt. A
regi-uj orosz allamfo rovid televizios beszedeben koszontotte a
lakossagot. Ramutatott: el kell kerulni a gyoztesek es a legyozottek
megkulonbozteteset, Oroszorszag egy haza, egy sors, egy jovo. A
kulfold megkonnyebbulessel fogadta Borisz Jelcin gyozelmet. A
Reuter azonban kiemeli: sokan aggodalmuknak adnak hangot, hogy
az elnok rossz egeszsegi allapota kepesse teszi-e arra, hogy a vilag
legnagyobb [teruletu] orszagat uj korszak fele kormanyozza (MH).

2. Hatarontul

[Autonomiatanacsot javasol a Fidesz-MDF-MDNP-KDNP]

Horn Gyula miniszterelnok tegnap a Parlamentben fogadta a hataron
tuli magyar szervezetek kepviseloit. Megerositette: a magyar
kormany a ketoldalu kapcsolatokban es a nemzetkozi forumokon
egyarant tamogatja a hataron tuli magyarsag jogainak garantalasara,
azok gyakorlati ervenyesitesere vonatkozo igenyeit. Tegnap a Fidesz-
MDF-MDNP-KDNP kozos dokumentumot adott ki, amelyben
javasoltak: jojjon letre autonomiatanacs, amelyben a resztvevok
kepviseloi foglalnak helyet, s amely rendszeresen ulesezik. E
szervezet feladata lenne a nemzetstrategia kidolgozasa, a magyar
kozossegi autonomiak kiepitesevel kapcsolatos feladatok
osszehangolasa politikai, diplomaciai, jogi, kulturalis es gazdasagi
teren. Orban Viktor szerint a kormanyzo partok ezzel az elkepzelessel
nem ertenek egyet. A negy part tamogatja a Magyarorszaggal
szomszedos allamok NATO-hoz es EU-hoz valo csatlakozasat, azzal a
feltetellel, hogy az illeto allam specialis, jogi statuson nyugvo
kozossegi autonomiat biztosit az ott elo magyar kozosseg szamara. Az
MSZP-SZDSZ szinten kozos nyilatkozatban erositette meg, hogy
hazank kulpolitikajaban az euroatlanti integracios celok mellett
egyidejuleg es azonos sullyal legyen kepviselve a szomszedsagi
kapcsolatok fejlesztese es a hataron tuli magyar kozossegek
tamogatasa. A kormanypartok tamogatjak a szomszedos allamok
NATO-hoz es EU-hoz torteno csatlakozasat, abban a remenyben, hogy
ezzel a demokracia zavartalan mukodese es a megfelelo
kisebbsegpolitika is ervenyesulni fog (MN).

3. Gazdasag

[Energiaaremeles - jon a hazudozas magasiskolaja]

Augusztusra igeri energiaaremelesi javaslatanak benyujtasat a
Magyar Energia Hivatal. Informaciok szerint elsokent tobb aremelesi
szintet - koztuk 20 szazalek alattit is - kivannak az Ipari es
Kereskedelmi Miniszterium ele terjeszteni aszerint, hogy mit
ismernek el a vallalatok torvenyben nem kelloen tisztazott
"legkisebb koltsegekent". Az IKM-nek es a kormanynak szeptember
vegeig kell dontenie az oktober elsejei aremeles mertekerol (MH).
[Jo mi? Azt mondjak "tobb aremelesi szint lesz - koztuk 20% alatti is"
Sok olvasoban persze csak annyi ragad meg, hogy 20% alatt marad az
emeles. FRASZKARIKAT! "Tobb szintrol volt szo" - mondjak majd. A

[Szocialis erzeketlenseg - legyen csak minel dragabb a kenyer]

Csobanczy Peter, az FM helyettes allamtitkara a NSZ-nak elmondta:

[Szaporodnak a havi tobbmillios fizetesu bankelnokok es alelnokok]

4. Belfold

[Elfogadas elott a zsido karpotlas, CSAK AZT NEM KOZLIK, MEKKORA
AZ OSSZEG! ]

Peldaerteku a zsido szervezetek es a kormany illetekes bizottsaganak
legutobbi megallapodasa egy kozalapitvany letrehozasarol - mondta
Zoltai Gusztav, a MAZSIHISZ ugyvezeto igazgatoja. A zsido
karpotlasrol a parlament osz elejen targyal. Komoroczy Geza, az ELTE
professzora szerint tajekozatlansagbol fakadnak es talan
rosszhiszemu tulzasok azok a kijelentesek, amelyek szerint a magyar
nemzeti vagyonnal volna egyenerteku az az osszeg, amely a zsido
kozossegeket a masodik vilaghaboru alatt elkobzott tulajdon
kerteritesekent megilleti. Komoroczy szerint magatol ertetodo, hogy a
tortenelem lenyegeben karpotlasok sorozata, es ki kell mondani: "...
az allami segedlettel vegrehajtott gyilkossagok aran keletkezett
vagyon igazsagos karpotlasarol es rendezeserol targyalasokat kell
folytatni" (MH).

[Pufajkas "kormanyulese": vesztes haborukat folytatnak]

A heten bejelentett kenyeraremeles elfogadhatatlan es
megalapozatlan, legfeljebb 2 forintos emeles indokolt - hangzott el a
kormany tegnapi ulesen [nos, majd csak tessenek figyelni, hogy lesz a
3,60-as kenyerbol 103,60, es a 2 forintos novekedesbol 22 forintos!]
A miniszterelnok utasitotta a foldmuvelesi, valamint az ipari es
kereskedelmi minisztert, hogy meg ezen a heten folytassanak
targyalasokat a merseklesrol a legnagyobb termelo es forgalmazo
cegekkel. A kerdessel a jovo heten ujra foglalkoznak. A kormanyfo
elkepzelese alapjan dontes szuletett a Kozponti Bunuldozesi
Igazgatosag letrehozasarol, amely a kozponti nyomozohivatal helyett
alakul, az ORFK-n belul. Feladata a szervezett bunozes visszaszoritasa
lesz. Az igazgatosag ugyanazokat a feladatokat fogja ellatni, mint
amiket a kozponti nyomozohivatal vegzett volna el. A kulonbseg
annyi, hogy mivel a rendorseg keretei kozott tevekenykedik majd,
nem lesz teljesen fuggetlen. [Vagyis, hogy Horn helyett Kuncze lesz a
fonok, vagyis az SZDSZ] Az uj szerv vezetoje a rendorfokapitany
egyik helyettese lesz. Az igazgatosag szeptember elsejen kezdi meg
mukodeset. A letesitesi es mukodesi feltetelek megteremtesere 680
millio forintot folyositanak a koltsegvetes altalanos tartalekabol. Az
elmult evekben elkezdodott lakasfinanszirozasi rendszer
atalakitasanak folytatasakent a kormany nem latja indokoltnak az
agazat kiemelt tamogatasat. A jelenlegi kozponti tamogatasi formak a
jovoben is fennmaradnak. Hosszabb tavon azonban egy
kolcsonfelvetelen alapulo onfinanszirozasi lakasrendszert kell
kialakitani, s a visszaszoritott tamogatasokat kizarolag a raszorultsag
aranyaban kell biztositani. A kabinet a parlament ele terjeszti a
lakas-takarekpenztarakrol szolo torveny tervezetet, a jovoben a
visszaelesek miatt megszigoritjak a lakascelu tamogatasok
igenybevetelenek lehetoseget. Mivel e tamogatasi forma reven
jelentosen megemelkedett 1995-ben (az elozo evhez kepest 44
szazalekkal nott) a sajat epitesu lakasok szama, igy tovabbra is
igenybe vehetoek a kedvezmenyek, de immar jelzaloggal biztositott
visszafizetesi kotelezettseggel terhelten. A kedvezmenyt akkor kell
visszafizetni, ha a lakast ot even belul a tulajdonos eladja vagy
elbontja. A szocialisan hatranyos helyzetu csaladok tamogatasara a
kormany a tarsadalmi szervezetek kiserleti programjat tamogatja,
ebben az evben mar 10 ezer lakas epitese kezdodhet. A kormany az
osszes lakascelu allami kiadas aranyat a koltsegvetesben 4
szazalekon kivanja stabilizalni (MH, MN, NSZ).

[Koalicios valsag]

Tobb lap foglalkozik azzal, hogy az elmult napokban ujra kielezodott
a koalicios partok kozotti viszony. A MH szerint a kozeli jovoben nem
varhato a Koalicios Egyezteto Tanacs osszehivasa az uj alkotmany
koncepcioja es a hazszabaly-modositas korul kialakult nezetelteres
miatt. Ez azonban nem zarja ki, hogy az ugynek sulyos
kovetkezmenyei legyenek a tovabbi koalicios egyuttmukodesre
nezve. Idezi a lap Horn Gyulat, aki felvetette a koalicios partok kozti
viszony ujragondolasat. Bauer Tamas, az SZDSZ ugyvivoje a lapnak
elmondta: az alkotmanyozas kudarca utan megromlott az SZDSZ es az
MSZP vezetoi kozti viszony. Ugy veli: a kozosen vallalt celok
vegrehajtasaert varhatoan 1998-ig fennmarad a koalicio, am
kerdeses, hogy helyreallithato-e a most megrendult bizalom a partok
kozott. A NSZV szerint egyik kormanypart vezeteseben sem hiszik,
hogy komolyabb kovetkezmenye lesz a legujabb koalicios
osszeutkozesnek, ezert mindket vezerkarban "hosszu hideg nyarra", a
nezetelteresek jegelesere szamitanak. Az MSZP-ben azonban tobben
ugy velik, at kell gondolni a koalicios egyuttmukodes nehany
szabalyat. Janosi Gyorgy alelnok ugy latja: az SZDSZ vezeteset irritalja,
hogy amig a szocialistak csokkeno tamogatottsaggal ugyan, de
stabilan elsok a lakossag koreben, addig az SZDSZ nepszerusege
folyamatosan alacsony. Ezert az SZDSZ megkiserli tagitani mozgasteret
a koalicion belul, felmutatva onallo politikai arculatat. Az is idegesiti
oket, hogy az ellenzeki partok mindig az SZDSZ-re zuditottak a
legnagyobb ossztuzet, ezt most oldani kell valemelyest. Janosi szerint
erre szolgalt a Nagy Imre-emlektorveny MSZP-s javaslatanak
elutasitasa, s a mostani ugy is. Ezert Janosi Gyorgy egyetert azzal az
elkepzelessel, hogy a ket part ne csupan kulon induljon az 1998-as
valasztasokon, hanem az MSZP mondja ki: ha jol szerepel, csak az
eredmeny ismereteben kezd koalicios targyalast. "Tamogatni fogok
olyan javaslatot, hogy elore ne kotelezzuk el magunkat az SZDSZ-szel
kotendo koalicio mellett" - mondotta Janosi Gyorgy. Az SZDSZ
vezetese a nyilvanossag elott nem kivan foglalkozni a tortentekkel.
Az UM-ban is tobben nyilatkoznak. Toller Laszlo (MSZP)
hangsulyozta: az MSZP szamara elfogadhatatlan az SZDSZ szerda esti
magatartasa a parlamentben, ennek ellenere ugy veli, nincs koalicios
valsag. Lanyi Zsolt (FKGP) szerint az lenne a legjobb, ha a kormany
lemondana es atadna a helyet egy olyan kabinetnek, amely a nemzet
erdekeit kepviseli, nem pedig parterdekeket. Trombitas Zoltan
(Fidesz) emlekeztet ra, hogy tavaly nyaron is volt valsag, "ugy tunik
a kormanykoalicio nehezen birja a meleget".

[Osszel folytatodik az orszaggyuloles a Parlamentben]

Az Orszaggyules 1996-ban 37 uj torvenyt es 29 torvenymodositast
fogadott el, a Haz az eredeti jogalkotasi program 70-80 szazalekat
teljesitette - kozolte tegnapi evadzaro sajtotajekoztatojan Gal Zoltan.
A hazelnok sajnalatosnak tartja, hogy a parlament szerdan nem
fogadta el a hazszabaly modositasat, mivel igy nem lephetett eletbe
szamos, a parlamenti munkat attekinthetove tevo es gyorsito
rendelkezes.

[Tessek csak figyelni, hogy szabotaljak el a kleptokratak a
vagyonnovekedesi vizsgalatot!]

Gal szerint a politikai elit onmagat jaratja le a kepviseloi
vagyonnyilatkozatok korul kialakult vitaval, s ezzel a politikai
stabilitast is veszelyezteti (MH). Osszegyultek a kepviselok
vagyongyarapodasat vizsgalo parlamenti bizottsag letrehozasahoz
szukseges alairasok - jelentette be Lanyi Zsolt, az FKGP frakciojanak
szovivoje tegnapi sajtotajekoztatojan. Lapertesulesek szerint az FKGP
kezdemenyezesehez eddig 79 kepviselo csatlakozott - 78 alairasra
volt szukseg -, a KDNP, a Fidesz es az MDF frakciojanak tobbsege, a
fuggetlenektol Fur Lajos es Salamon Laszlo. Allitolag e negy
kepviselocsoport prominens szemelyisegei kozul csak Boross
Peternek (MDF), az elozo kormany miniszterelnokenek az alairasa
nem szerepel az iveken. A 79 kepviselo kozott nincs egyetlen MDNP-
s es kormanyparti sem. A bizottsag megalakitasa a parlament oszi
ulesszakanak kezdeten lehetseges. Majtenyi Laszlo adatvedelmi
biztos arra hivta fel a figyelmet, hogy jelenleg teljesen rendezetlen a
kepviseloi vagyonnyilatkozatteteli kotelezettsegekkel kapcsolatos
szabalyozas. A kepviselok jogallasarol szolo torveny ugy rendelkezik,
hogy minden kepviselonek ketszer, a mandatuma kezdeten es vegen
kell vagyonnyilatkozatot tennie, es ez csak az erintett
hozzajarulasaval hozhato nyilvanossagra (NSZ).

[72%-os tobbseggel sem tud kormanyozni a kleptokrata banda]

Az MDF szerint a 72 szazalekos parlamenti tobbseggel biro koalicio
torvenyhozasi munkaja lehetett volna hatekonyabb is. Gemesi
Gyorgy ugyvezeto elnok tegnap kijelentette: sajnalattal veszik
tudomasul, hogy a kormany 33 torvenyjavaslatot nem terjesztett elo
a Haz szerdan befejezodott tavaszi-nyari ulesszakaban. Gemesi
Gyorgy ugy itelte meg, hogy a kormanypartoknak osztol
komolyabban kell majd venni feladataikat. Az MDF szerint a tragikus
parlamenti dontesek nyoman egyre nehezebb penzugyi helyzetbe
kerulnek az onkormanyzatok is. Az ugyvezeto elnok felhivta a
figyelmet arra, hogy az onkormanyzatok meg mindig nem kaptak
meg azokat az APV Rt. altal megigert reszvenycsomagokat, amelyek
az energiaszolgaltatok privatizacios bevetelebol jar nekik. Lehoczky
Ferenc, az MDF elnoksegi tagja kozolte: az onkormanyzatok tobb mint
fele birosaghoz fordult az ugyben (MH).

[Szekszualis gimnasztikara koltottek az egeszsegbiztositas penzet]

Az egeszsegbiztosito gazdalkodasat vizsgalo parlamenti bizottsag es
az Allami Szamvevoszek szerint parttalan, esetenkent torvenytelen
volt a betegsegek megelozeset celzo, ketmilliard forintot meghalado
palyazati penzek szetosztasa. Az ASZ szerint a betegsegek
megelozesere szant palyazati penz resze az egeszsegbiztosito
gyogyitasi celu kiadasainak, igy beruhazasokra nem, kizarolag
mukodesi celokra fordithato. Vizsgalatuk soran kiderult, hogy az
elnyert tamogatast epitkezesre, orvosi gep-muszer beszerzesere,
szemelygepkocsi- es butorvasarlasra forditottak. Loterapiara es
szexualis gimnasztikara is jutott az osszegbol. A bizottsag tegnapi
ulesen bejelentettek: a Borsod megyei rendorseg kulonosen nagy kart
okozo csalas miatt folytatja a nyomozast a biztositoval maganmento-
szallitasra szerzodott Porkolab es tarsai ellen (NSZ).

[Teljes anarchia a kozoktatasban]

A Pedagogusok Szakszervezete elhibazottnak tartja a kozoktatasi
torveny modositasanak tobb pontjat. Az orszagos vezetoseg
allasfoglalasat Szollosi Istvanne fotitkar jelentette be tegnap az ulest
koveto sajtotajekoztaton. A PSZ szerint a torvenymodositas nem
szolgalja a szfera fejlodeset. Teljes anarchiat okoz az a dontes,
amelyik a tanarok megemelt kotelezo oraszamanak a bevezeteset -
harom lehetseges idoponttal - az iskolafenntarto onkormanyzatokra
bizta. Szollosine nyilvanvalonak tartja, hogy a tobbsegeben
forrashiannyal kuszkodo onkormanyzatok az oraszamemeles
szeptemberi bevezetese es igy a foloslegesse valo pedagogusok
elbocsatasa mellett dontenek. A PSZ a tanevinditassal egyidejuleg
megkezdi a szuksegesnek itelt valaszlepesek, akciok szervezeset. A
Magyar Egyetemi es Foiskolai Hallgatok Orszagos Szovetsege szerint a
modositott felsooktatasi torveny alapjaiban hibas. A MEFHOSZ
furcsallja, hogy mig Dinya Laszlo helyettes allamtitkar az
egeszsegugyi foiskolak hallgatoi letszamemelese szuksegessegerol
beszel, ekozben otezer allast szuntetnek meg az egeszsegugyben.
Bercsenyi Laszlo elnok furcsallja Magyar Balint nyilatkozatat, hogy az
elbocsatott tanarokat adoellenorre kepeznek at. Szerinte talan inkabb
a kormanytagokat kellene nemikepp atkepezni (UM, NSZ).

[Sztrajk keszul a kozszolgalatban]

Alkotmanybirosaghoz fordulnak a hetvegi kamionstop megszuntetese

[TV-elnokseg manipulacioja]

A MN szerint egyes politikai erok megprobalnak nyomast gyakorolni
az MTV kuratoriumanak elnoksegere, hogy az intezmeny elnokenek
megvalasztasara kiirt masodik fordulo is eredmenytelenul zaruljon, s
harmadszorra egy idaig hivatalosan nem palyazo tekintelyes
szemelyiseget - minden bizonnyal Gazso Ferencet - juttassak a
teveelnoki szekbe. Allitolag errol mar elvi megegyezes szuletett

sugarzasanak kotelezo aranyat legalabb 51 szazalekban hatarozza
meg valamennyi hazai teveallomas szamara. Ertesulesek szerint a

jogszabalyon (MH).

Oriasi kozhely lenne azt mondani, hogy a magyar foci rossz. Na nem

YELTSIN WINS CONVINCING VICTORY IN RUNOFF. President Boris
Yeltsin won a
strong victory in the second round voting on 3 July. With 97% of the
vote counted through the unofficial electronic vote counting system
at
midday Moscow time on 4 July, the results, according to ITAR-TASS
and
the National News Service, were:

Turnout 67.25% 73 million
Boris Yeltsin 53.70% 38,936,208
Zyuganov 40.41% 29,301,089
Against All 4.86% 3.5 million

The final tally will take several days to complete. -- Robert Orttung

YELTSIN ADDRESSES COUNTRY AFTER VICTORY... In a television
address 4 July, Yeltsin saluted Russia for carrying out the elections
and showing the world that "Russia is a great country." He stressed
that Russians should not be divided into winners and losers, and
called on everyone to work together, ITAR-TASS reported. Yeltsin
looked more relaxed and spoke more fluently than he had on 1 July,
Reuters reported. The president began working in his Kremlin
residence at 8:30 a.m. on 4 July, according to spokesman Sergei
Medvedev. -- Robert Orttung

-AND TAPS CHERNOMYRDIN TO FORM NEW GOVERNMENT. Prime
Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin announced that Yeltsin had asked him
to form a new government, ITAR-TASS reported on 4 July. Duma
Speaker Gennadii Seleznev said on 4 July that the Duma would
confirm Chernomyrdin, ITAR-TASS reported. Yeltsin campaign aide
Vyacheslav Nikonov told OMRI that he expected Chernomyrdin to be
confirmed since the Communist Party does not want to give Yeltsin
an opportunity to dissolve the Duma. According to the constitution,
Yeltsin can disband the Duma if it rejects his choice for prime
minister three times. Chernomyrdin was not approved by the Duma
elected in 1993 since the constitution adopted then stipulated that
the prime minister only needed approval after presidential elections.
-- Robert Orttung with Laura Belin in Moscow

ADVISERS SEE NEXT CABINET AS "COALITION OF PROFESSIONALS"...
Presidential advisers on 3 and 4 July were united in predicting that
Yeltsin will invite representatives from a wide range of political
groups into the new government. However, Sergei Filatov, Georgii
Satarov, and others were quick to point out that the new cabinet
would be a "coalition of professionals," not just a selection of people
from different political parties. Andranik Migranyan told OMRI that
he expects Zyuganov's party to split soon, after which several
"moderate communists" could join the government. Former
communist "reserve" presidential candidate Aman Tuleev was
frequently mentioned as a possible cabinet member. Vyacheslav
Nikonov also mentioned Democratic Party of Russia leader Sergei
Glazev, who ran for parliament last year on the Congress of Russian
Communities ticket. Nikonov told OMRI that the president will spend
about six weeks holding consultations before the final composition of
the new government is determined. -- Laura Belin in Moscow

-BUT LEBED'S ROLE UNCLEAR. Security Council Secretary Aleksandr
Lebed was scheduled to meet with reporters at the Yeltsin
campaign's press center on 3 July, but he did not appear. As he voted
on 3 July, Lebed reiterated his wish that General Igor Rodionnov be
appointed Defense Minister, NTV reported. When asked whether a
stricter visa regime will soon be imposed against foreigners, as Lebed
suggested last week, Vyacheslav Nikonov told OMRI that Lebed said
many things during the campaign to try to win his electorate over to
Yeltsin's side. Not all of these pronouncements will become policy,
Nikonov added. He suggested that Lebed's ideas on economic policy
were less likely to be implemented than his program for fighting
crime and corruption. Commenting on Lebed's recent hints that he
would like to become vice president under Yeltsin, several advisers
including Nikonov and Migranyan said the post will not be revived
because the Constitution is so difficult to amend. -- Laura Belin in
Moscow

PRESIDENT'S TEAM EVASIVE ON HEALTH ISSUE. When President
Yeltsin voted early in the day on 3 July, only television cameras
connected directly to his campaign were all allowed at the site. The
state-run channels ORT and RTR broadcast an edited videotape in
which it appeared that Yeltsin had trouble placing his ballot in the
box. Yeltsin did not appear at his campaign's press center on the
evening of 3 July. Advisers and campaign representatives insisted
that the president was suffering from a cold or bronchitis and
indirectly denied that he might have heart trouble. Adviser Georgii
Satarov said he expected Yeltsin to rest somewhere outside Moscow
in the coming weeks. Satarov emphasized that Yeltsin will continue
to work, sign decrees, and hold consultations. -- Laura Belin in
Moscow

FORMER CANDIDATES VOTE AGAINST BOTH. Former candidates
Mikhail Gorbachev and Vladimir Zhirinovsky voted against both
Yeltsin and Zyuganov, the BBC reported. Gorbachev said that the best
alternative would be for Russia to hold new elections. When the
results were clear, Zhirinovsky recommended that Zyuganov take up
diplomatic work as the Russian ambassador to Cuba. Grigorii
Yavlinskii refused to reveal whom he had voted for, but expressed
the hope that the majority of his supporters would back Yeltsin. With
Yeltsin's victory obvious, Yavlinskii said that he believed life would
continue as it had earlier, NTV reported. -- Robert Orttung

OPPOSITION ACCEPTS DEFEAT. With Yeltsin's victory apparent,
Zyuganov said that he still supported the idea of a coalition
government, AFP reported on 3 July. Deputy Duma Speaker Sergei
Baburin described Yeltsin's victory as one of "fear over conscience,"
Russian TV reported on 3 July. People feared that if Yeltsin lost, he
would start a civil war, Baburin explained. Zyuganov advisor Aleksei
Podberezkin claimed that Zyuganov's popular-patriotic bloc had
demonstrated an unprecedented durability over the six-month
campaign. He also noted that Yeltsin had adopted many of their
ideas, particularly dumping Foreign Minister Andrei Kozyrev and in
economic and social policy. Working Russia leader Viktor Anpilov
said that he would not call his people onto the streets, arguing that it
was impossible to falsify the election results and that conducting a
civil war in Russia was also impossible. He said, however, that "we
will fight" if the price of food rises and the workers cannot afford it.
Chairman of the Duma Legislative Committee Anatolii Lukyanov told
AFP that the communists would demand key posts in the
government, pointing out that less important posts would discredit
the idea of a coalition. Communist campaign manager Valentin
Kuptsov blamed the lower turnout in the second round on the
"hysteria" raised by Yeltsin campaign. He also accused the Central
Electoral Commission of violating the law by handing out numerous
certificates allowing citizens to vote in areas where they are not
registered, Ekho Moskvy reported on 3 July. -- Robert Orttung

INTERNATIONAL REACTIONS. Western capitals reacted positively to
Yeltsin's apparent victory, but worries about the Russian president's
health tempered the optimism of many observers. U.S. President Bill
Clinton on 3 July hailed the election as a "triumph of democracy."
Clinton's likely Republican opponent, former Senator Bob Dole,
congratulated Yeltsin, but said democracy is not built "on any one
election," and cautioned that Yeltsin faces "many challenges" ahead.
In Brussels, NATO officials termed Yeltsin's win "reassuring," adding
they hoped for a better relationship with Moscow now that the
elections are over. British Prime Minister John Major said the election
was a sign that "democracy has taken firm root in Russia." Tokyo also
expressed satisfaction with Yeltsin's victory, but said his uncertain
health "is reason for worry." Attention will now focus on Yeltsin's
choices for his new cabinet, and the role of Security Council Secretary
Aleksandr Lebed. -- Scott Parrish

INTERNATIONAL OBSERVERS JUDGE ELECTION "FREE AND FAIR..." At
a Moscow press conference on 4 July, international election observers
from the OSCE, the Council of Europe, and the European Parliament,
declared that the 3 July runoff had been "free, unbiased, and fair,"
Russian and Western agencies reported. As in the first round, the
international observers issued their verdict before the final results of
the election had been released. The heads of all three delegations
said they had seen no evidence of "manipulation or falsification"
during the voting, although they observed some minor irregularities.
About 1,300 international observers participated in monitoring on
election day. U.S. State Department spokesman Nicholas Burns also
characterized the election as "fairly contested." However, Konstanze
Krehl, head of the European Parliament observers, noted that media
coverage during the campaign had been heavily biased in favor of
Yeltsin, especially between the first and second rounds, when
Zyuganov received unrelenting negative coverage on national
television and radio networks. Some independent Western experts
disagreed with the official assesment of the election campaign.
According to Ekho Moskvy on 3 July, foreign observers from the
European Media Institute noted 50 to 60 media violations, all in
favor of Yeltsin. Dmitrii Simes of the Nixon Center in Washington
called the campaign "highly deplorable," arguing that Yeltsin had a
monopoly of television and radio coverage, and used state funds and
to finance his campaign. If former U.S. President George Bush had
had such advantages in 1992, said Simes, he would have won re-
election. -- Scott Parrish

-BUT ELECTORAL IRREGULARITIES NOTED... Although the second
round of the elections were generally regarded as free and fair, a
number of violations were noted. The pro-Yeltsin media reported a
variety of minor irregularities, mostly in favor of Zyuganov.
Presidential aide Georgii Satarov told Ekho Moskvy that a wave of
"brazen and unprecedented violations" were reported in the "red
belt" areas. He said that in Vladimir Oblast there were up to 15
mobile ballot boxes at some polling stations, although no applications
had been made to vote outside the polling station. Radio Mayak and
ITAR-TASS reported that there was some direct campaigning on 2
July in support of Gennadii Zyuganov. Copies of Pravda were found in
letterboxes in Moscow, for example, while communist party activists
distributed leaflets in Komi and Bashkortostan, the reports said. In
Rostov-na-donu, the procurator said copes of ballot slips marked for
Zyuganov were found in the letterboxes of voters in one part of his
city. -- Penny Morvant

BALTIC VOTERS SUPPORT ZYUGANOV, OTHER RUSSIANS ABROAD
PREFER YELTSIN. As in the first round on16 June, a high proportion

BIZARRE VOTING INCIDENTS. A number of odd incidents occurred on
election day, according to ITAR-TASS. In Vladivostok a group of
youngsters tried to set fire to a polling station, but their ire was
directed against a commercial outlet in the same building rather than
voters. In Perm and Rostov-na-donu voting was disrupted by bomb
scares, while in St. Petersburg a man was detained for threatening to
blow up a city railway station if he did not receive $10,000. -- Penny
Morvant

POLLSTERS MADE ACCURATE PREDICTIONS. Sociologists, who are
notorious in Russia for their wrong predictions of election outcomes,
were fairly close in their forecasts of the runoff results. None of the
Russian public opinion experts doubted President Yeltsin's reelection
and many foresaw almost exactly number of votes he received. The
most precise were VCIOM and Russian Independent Institute for
Social and National Problems (RNISiNP)- they predicted 53% support
for Yeltsin and 38-40% for Zyuganov. Both the Public Opinion
Foundation (FOM) and ROMIR polling agencies predicted 50% of the
vote for Yeltsin, but they differed over Zyuganov's support -- FOM
overestimated by giving him 46%, while ROMIR was more precise
with its prediction of 39%. Public Opinion Foundation was the most
precise in guessing how many people would vote against both
candidates -- 4%, while other agencies reckoned on about 7%. --
Anna Paretskaya

EFFORTS TO FORM ANTI-COMMUNIST DUMA BLOC TO CONTINUE...
Aleksandr Shokhin, a deputy chairman of the State Duma and a
leading member of Our Home Is Russia, told OMRI on 4 July that
efforts will continue to form an anti-communist bloc in the Duma, but
he emphasized that such a bloc would only be able to amass a
majority of votes on certain concrete issues. In principle, Our Home
Is Russia, the Russian Regions faction and some independent deputies
have agreed to join an anti-communist bloc, but not Grigorii
Yavlinskii's Yabloko. In addition, Shokhin said Vladimir Zhirinovsky's
Liberal Democratic Party of Russia occasionally could be recruited to
oppose the communists, but obviously could not be considered a
reliable ally. Shokhin was extremely skeptical regarding the
prospects of the All-Russian Democratic Union, which was formed
last week by Forward, Russia! leader Boris Fedorov, former Duma
Speaker Ivan Rybkin, and Party of Russian Unity and Concord leader
Sergei Shakhrai (see OMRI Special Report, 2 July 1996). Shokhin said
Our Home Is Russia will in all likelihood not join the new union. Also
on 4 July, Democratic Russia co-leader Galina Starovoitova told OMRI
her movement would definitely join the All-Russian Democratic
Union, but she doubted that the union would manage to persuade
most democratic movements to contest the next parliamentary
elections as a single bloc. -- Laura Belin in Moscow

-EVEN AS LEFT-WING DEPUTIES MAY BECOME EASIER TO WORK
WITH. According to Shokhin, the current parliament is likely to serve
out its term, and Communists will shun confrontation so as to
preserve their Duma apparatus as a base for future electoral efforts.
He also said he expects deputies from the Agrarian Party to become
more cooperative with the government, particularly when the Duma
resumes discussion of the land code, which was recently rejected by
the Federation Council. -- Laura Belin in Moscow

ADVISERS OFFER DIFFERENT ASSESSMENTS OF MEDIA'S ROLE. When
asked about the role of the media in the campaign, presidential
advisers not surprisingly praised the contribution made by pro-
Yeltsin journalists. Mark Urnov, who directed Yeltsin's analytical
center before leaving to work full time within campaign structures,
suggested that the Russian mass media had improved their
professionalism during the campaign. He argued that before January
1996, Russia had only an opposition press -- either left-wing (pro-
communist) or liberal. Since January 1996, Urnov claimed, the media
had taken a great step forward toward a "normal, democratic and
stable" institution by both criticizing and supporting the president.
(In fact, criticism of Yeltsin in the liberal media was almost entirely
absent during the months before the election.) Andranik Migranyan
disagreed with Urnov; he argued that it was "naive" to believe that
the current relationship between the media and authorities is
normal. Georgii Satarov admitted that the media had not been
objective during the campaign, but said it was unfair for critics to
compare Russia to developed western democracies in which elections
do not threaten the entire political system. He noted that democratic
procedures had allowed figures like Hitler to come to power. Given
the fact that these elections threatened to reverse Russia's
democratic progress, Satarov argued, it was not merely justifiable
but necessary for the media to work for the president in the short
term in order to protect their independence in the long term. When
asked by one journalist whether the media should primarily report
the news rather than do propaganda work for one candidate, Satarov
replied angrily that Russia's three main television networks
themselves decided that in order to preserve their ability to report
the news in the future, they had to do "propaganda work" this spring.
-- Laura Belin in Moscow
